chasing beauty

2026-06-03

This has gone on long enough that I am ready to call myself a writer, making no claims on whether I am a good one or not. At least as the pastime of a retired gentleman, as I seem to be willing to publicly admit these days.

Note: While the NJKpopper posts now being ported over here maintain their original illustrations, I’m going to revert to the original sparer, text style of this blog for new posts.

If a famous director like Bertolucci can use “Stealing Beauty” as a title, I can perhaps get away with “Chasing Beauty” as a title here, despite finding both a bit cringe, or in older lingo, cheesy.

When I now reread my older posts and other stories and notes “for the drawer”, I am quite satisfied. Even when I don’t find them good, they express what I wanted them to express, and I am still after the same things.

It appears that my only theme is perhaps too traditional to be successfully published or at least recognized today. That is the intersection of desire, seeking after beauty and the eternal, from the point of view of the male gaze. Except that I seek to smash it up and reassemble it, kintsugi-style. Not in the trendy, fashionable sense of kintsugi, but in a more elemental way, using the cringe elements to force a confrontation or recognition of the real. I am neither looking for a classical ideal nor a traditional “épater la bourgeoisie”, but simply seeking to highlight just a few moments in my own distinctive way. Having lived long enough, I believe that there are just a handful of really significant events in a person’s lifetime that form pivots of focus. In my case, as empirically proven, those are all wrapped up in desire for a (female) soul, a (female) voice, a (female) intellect. If Kawabata could get away with honing in obsessively on this theme, I might as well give it a shot.

But this is a different age, and there is therefore a lot to unpack, most importantly the female gaze that is returned upon the male gazer, and its reciprocal impact. This is partly why I have heaved over Zola in favor of Duras, Houellebecq in favor of Despentes, Blier in favor of Varda.

It is pointless to continue beyond this into a dull explication of how the reassembled smash up is intended to work. Better to just finish the story I am working on.
